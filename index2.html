About

My first taste of bicycle fabrication began I was 13. My dad and I would make bar ends and seat post clamps on the mill and lathe, and I took them to my local bike shop to trade for other parts. Taking those pieces of metal and turning them into something that could actually be used on a bicycle really got me interested in fabrication. From there, I would make anything involving metalwork. This included everything from roll cages to furniture. Once I was in college, I was lucky enough to get a job welding for a BMX company. I learned by welding sub-assemblies for frames, handlebars, seat posts, general frame fabrication, and machining.
